Anti-conservative postings - 1

This is the first conversation thread added to Exhibit B, revolving around a Kermit meme stating "I think all of Trump's supporters are deplorable for backing an openly racist candidate but that's none of my business."

While I think it's dangerous to cast a group I disagree with, with the same idealogical brush I don't think this first thread is harrassment in any way. The meme's creator is part of quite a large mindset that believe without nuance that all Trump supporters must be white supremacists or at least share their values. Myself and hopefully many of those that read this know this not to be true as people voted for Trump for a plethora of reasons (such as...).

Admittedly the media and Hillary Clinton herself have played a huge role in portraying both Trump and his supporters' image. For example, I don't think Trump would have been associated with the word deplorable to any popular extent if it had not been for Hillary Clinton's "Basket of Deplorables" quote at a LGBT fundraiser on September 9th, 2016. The quote is notable enough to have its own Wikipedia page.

"You know, to just be grossly generalistic, you could put half of Trump's supporters into what I call the basket of deplorables. Right? They’re racist, sexist, homophobic, xenophobic — Islamophobic — you name it. And unfortunately, there are people like that. And he has lifted them up. He has given voice to their websites that used to only have 11,000 people — now have 11 million. He tweets and retweets their offensive hateful mean-spirited rhetoric. Now, some of those folks — they are irredeemable, but thankfully, they are not America."

Having read the quote through several times, my only real criticism use of the word "half" which Hillary Clinton herself admitted was an incorrect use of words in her book What Happened. I think the real number of Trump supporters that fit this demographic are far lower and much exaggerated.

As stated previously, I have no legal expertise but I from what little I do know such as the 1st Amendment offering freedom of speech to plenty of crazy ideas. I'd argue that this section is the weakest part of the lawsuit as a whole.
